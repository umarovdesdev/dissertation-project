CSL improves minority-class performance and reduces high-cost misclassification errors.

### Generalization claims

CSL preserves data distribution and can outperform resampling in some domains.

### Clinical applicability claims

CSL is particularly suitable for medical scenarios where false negatives are clinically severe.

### Superiority claims

CSL offers computational efficiency compared to resampling techniques .

# 18. Analytical Synthesis (5–8 sentences)

This article serves as a foundational methodological review establishing cost-sensitive learning as a structured research domain within medical AI. It demonstrates that imbalance mitigation is a dominant research theme, particularly in diagnostic contexts. However, it reveals a field heavily reliant on retrospective historical-based evaluation, with limited prospective or multi-center validation. The absence of meta-analytic synthesis weakens quantitative generalization strength. For a diabetic retinopathy dissertation, the paper supports the argument that imbalance handling is critical but does not provide architecture-level benchmarking evidence. It strengthens justification for rigorous external validation and cross-dataset evaluation. It does not challenge preprocessing dominance, as preprocessing effects are not isolated or compared at scale.
