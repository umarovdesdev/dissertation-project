## 13 assessment criteria

1. **The topic matches** the directions of science development / state programmes.
2. **Importance for science** (relevance, significance of the topic).
3. **Principle of independence** — the work was carried out by the candidate independently.
4. **Principle of internal unity** (sub-items 4.1–4.5): integrity of the work, links between chapters,
   unified logic, conclusions matching the objectives, absence of contradictions.
5. **Principle of scientific novelty** (sub-items 5.1–5.3): novelty of the formulation, methods, results.
6. **Validity of the main conclusions** — conclusions follow from the results.
7. **Main statements submitted for defense** (7.1–7.5: assessment of each statement separately).
8. **Principle of reliability** (8.1–8.5): correctness of methods, data, experiments,
   statistical processing, reproducibility.
9. **Principle of practical value** (9.1–9.3): applicability, implementation, economic effect.
10. **Quality of writing and formatting** (compliance with [[../02-formatting/gost-formatting|GOST]]).
11. **Comments** (list of critical comments — in italics).
12. **Scientific level of the articles** (compliance with publication requirements — WoS/Scopus, KKSON;
    see [[../00-regulatory-framework/publication-requirements]]).
13. **Decision of the official reviewer** — the final conclusion on the possibility of awarding the
    doctor of philosophy (PhD) degree under educational programme <...>.
