# Template: PhD dissertation structure

> **Purpose:** a qualification research work for the degree of doctor of philosophy (PhD),
> prepared independently (with a scientific consultant) under an educational programme of field 8D061.
> **Formatting:** strictly per [[../02-formatting/gost-formatting|GOST 7.32-2001 / 7.1-2003]]
> (up to 300 pp., font size 14, single spacing, margins 30/20/10/20 mm).

## Content requirement (§3.1.1 of the Instruction)

A doctoral dissertation must contain **new, scientifically substantiated results** that either solve
a major scientific/applied problem or constitute a major achievement in the development of a
specific scientific direction.

## Structural elements (in order)

1. **Title page** — organization, UDC, full name, work title, programme code and name,
   sought degree, scientific consultant, city/year; candidate's personal signature.
2. **Contents** — introduction, all sections/subsections/items with titles, conclusion,
   list of references, appendices, with page numbers.
3. **Normative references** — "This dissertation uses references to the following standards".
4. **Definitions** — terms with definitions.
5. **Designations and abbreviations** — in a column, with decoding (may be merged with "Definitions").
6. **Introduction** — see below.
7. **Main body** — sections, subsections, items.
8. **Conclusion** — see below.
9. **List of references used** — in order of appearance, per GOST 7.1-2003.
10. **Appendices** — implementation acts, certificates, listings, additional data.

## Content of the "Introduction" (§5.6)

The introduction must reflect:
- an assessment of the current state of the scientific/technological problem being solved;
- the basis and initial data, justification of the need for the research;
- the **relevance** and **novelty** of the topic; links with other research;
- the **aim**, **object** and **subject** of the research, the **objectives**;
- the **methodological basis**;
- the **statements submitted for defense**.

## Content of the "Main body" (§5.7)

- choice and justification of the research direction, methods for solving the objectives and their
  comparative assessment, the general methodology;
- the process of theoretical and/or experimental research (methods, calculations, operating
  principles of the developed objects, their characteristics);
- generalization and assessment of the results: completeness of solving the problem, assessment of
  **reliability**, comparison with domestic and foreign analogues, proposals for further directions.

## Content of the "Conclusion" (§5.8)

- brief conclusions on the results;
- assessment of the completeness of solving the set objectives;
- recommendations and initial data for the specific use of the results;
- assessment of the technical and economic effectiveness of implementation;
- assessment of the scientific level of the work compared with the best achievements in the field.
