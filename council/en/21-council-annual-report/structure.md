# Template: Report on the work of the dissertation council (Appendix 1)

> **Purpose:** the council's annual report (Appendix 1 of the Statute). Prepared at year-end.
> **Signed by:** the Chairman + Academic secretary of the dissertation council + seal, date.
> This is a council document (not the candidate's), but knowing its structure is useful for
> understanding how dissertations are described (section 4 sets the "reference" description of a work).

## Header

```
REPORT
on the work of the Dissertation Council of IITU ("International Information Technology University")
in the field of training 8D061 — Information and Communication Technologies
```

Preamble: the basis (order of the Chairman of the Committee for Quality Assurance… establishing the
council), the council chairman, the list of specialties/programmes (8D06101, 8D06102,
6D070300/8D06103, 8D06105).

## Structure (7 sections)

1. **Data on the number of sessions held.** A numbered list of all sessions for the year
   (admission to defense + defense: candidate's name, topic, educational programme).
2. **Names of council members who attended fewer than half of the sessions.** (Often: "None".)
3. **List of doctoral candidates with their HEI** — table:

   | No. | Full name | Specialty | Dissertation topic | Scientific consultants | Institution of study |
   |-----|-----------|-----------|--------------------|------------------------|----------------------|

4. **Brief analysis of the dissertations** considered by the council, highlighting:
   - analysis of the topics of the works considered;
   - the link of the topics with the directions of science development set by the VNTK under the
     Government of the RK (subpara. 2 para. 3 art. 20 of the Law of the RK "On Science and Technology
     Policy") and/or state programmes;
   - analysis of the level of implementation of the results in practice.
   For each work, usually: object/subject, results obtained, implementation acts, alignment with
   priorities, theoretical and practical significance.
5. **Analysis of the work of official reviewers** (with examples of the lowest-quality reports).
6. **Proposals for further improvement of the system of training scientific personnel.**
7. **Number of PhD dissertations by profile, broken down by directions of training:**
   1) admitted to defense (incl. candidates from other HEIs);
   2) withdrawn from consideration;
   3) with negative reviewer reports;
   4) with a negative decision following the defense;
   5) sent for revision;
   6) sent for re-defense.
