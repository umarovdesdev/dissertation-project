## Key quantitative rules

### Council composition (§2)
- The council is created for a term of **3 years**.
- No more than **7 members**; of these **at least 3** are from other universities/organizations.
- **4 permanent** members (including the chairman, deputy, academic secretary) + **3 temporary**.
- **At least 4 members** must have **≥3 publications** in **Q1–Q3 JCR** journals (Web of Science)
  or with a **CiteScore percentile (Scopus) ≥ 35**.

### Admission to defense (§4.2)
The candidate submits to the academic secretary a set of documents (dissertation,
abstract/annotation, list of papers, consultants' reviews, ethics committee conclusion,
candidate information, etc.). Each document is described by a separate template in folders
`10`–`21` of this knowledge base.

### Preliminary review (§5)
- Held at an **extended meeting** of the relevant department/council.
- **2 reviewers** are appointed.
- §5.4 defines the content of the **conclusion** of the preliminary review.
- Details — [[../01-defense-process/preliminary-review]].

### Voting (§7.9–7.11)
- Secret ballot; the decision to award the degree requires a majority of **at least 3/4** of the
  council members present.
- Details — [[../01-defense-process/voting-and-appeal]].
