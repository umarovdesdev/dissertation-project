# Publication requirements for the PhD candidate

> What the candidate must publish, and where, before the defense. These requirements define the
> structure of the [[../18-list-of-publications/structure|list of scientific papers]] and are
> checked by official reviewers (criterion 12 in [[../15-official-reviewer-report/structure]]).

## Publication categories (as grouped in the list of papers)

The candidate's list of scientific papers is built by fixed groups — in exactly this order:

1. **Publications in journals indexed in the international information resources
   Web of Science (Clarivate Analytics) and Scopus.**
   For each article, the **quartile** (Q1/Q2/Q3/Q4) and/or **CiteScore percentile** is given.
2. **Publications in journals recommended by KKSON MSHE RK** (see [[kkson-journal-list]]).
3. **Publications in proceedings of international scientific-practical conferences.**
4. **Copyright certificates / certificates of state registration**
   (computer programs, databases) — NIIS/NIIP of the Ministry of Justice RK.

## Quality thresholds (from the Statute)

- WoS/Scopus articles are assessed by **quartile** (Q1 highest) and **CiteScore percentile**.
- Publication threshold for council members: ≥3 articles in Q1–Q3 JCR or CiteScore percentile ≥35
  (see [[council-composition]]) — this also serves as a quality benchmark for the candidate.

## Mandatory publication requirement for the candidate (order No. 230)

Per para. 6 of the Rules for Awarding Academic Degrees (order of the MES RK No. 127 of 31.03.2011,
amended by order No. 230 of 24.05.2019; clarified by the Committee's instructive letter No. 19-7/471
of 31.05.2019), at least one article on the dissertation topic must be published in an
international peer-reviewed journal that meets **one** of the following conditions:

- is indexed in **Scopus** with a **CiteScore percentile ≥ 25** in at least one scientific area
  matching the content of the dissertation; **or**
- is indexed in the **Web of Science Core Collection** in the sections **Science Citation Index
  Expanded (SCIE)**, **Social Sciences Citation Index (SSCI)** or **Arts and Humanities Citation
  Index (AHCI)**.

> This is the minimum threshold for the candidate (≥25); do not confuse it with the council
> members' threshold (≥35). The amendment applies to cohorts whose extended departmental meeting
> (pre-defense) was held after order No. 230 came into force.

## Anti-plagiarism

The dissertation undergoes a borrowings (plagiarism) check via **NCSTE**.
