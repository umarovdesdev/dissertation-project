# Voting and appeal

> Sections 7 (§7.9–7.11) and 8 of the Statute on the council.

## Voting (§7.9–7.11)

- Voting is by **secret ballot**.
- The decision to award the **doctor of philosophy (PhD)** degree is taken if **at least 3/4** of the
  council members present at the session vote in favour.
- The quorum and the composition of those voting are recorded by the
  [[../20-attendance-sheet/structure|attendance sheet]] (Appendix 4).
- Results are formalized by minutes and the council's conclusion.

## Possible decisions following the defense

(categories for the annual count — see [[../21-council-annual-report/structure]]):

1. dissertations admitted to defense;
2. withdrawn from consideration;
3. with negative reviewer reports;
4. with a negative decision following the defense;
5. sent for revision;
6. sent for re-defense.

## Appeal (§8)

Section 8 of the Statute establishes the procedure for filing and considering an **appeal** against
the dissertation council's decision (deadlines, the body reviewing it, consequences).
