# Formatting the dissertation and abstract (GOST)

> Per the "Instruction on formatting the dissertation and abstract" (order of the Chairman of the
> HAC of the MES RK No. 377-3zh of 28.09.2004), based on **GOST 7.32-2001** and **GOST 7.1-2003**.
> Uniform rules for all dissertations and abstracts in the RK. Applies to the
> [[../10-dissertation/structure|dissertation]] and the [[../11-abstract-annotation/structure|abstract]].

## Length

| Work | Length |
|------|--------|
| Doctoral dissertation (PhD) | as a rule, **up to 300 pages** |
| Candidate dissertation | up to 150 pages |
| Humanities | +25% to the above |
| Doctoral abstract | up to 2 printer's sheets (humanities — up to 2.5) |

> Appendices are **not included** in the dissertation's page count.

## Page and font parameters (§6.1)

- Format — **A4**, text on **one side** of the sheet.
- Line spacing — **single (1)**.
- Font — regular, **size 14**.
- Margins: **right — 10 mm, top — 20 mm, left — 30 mm, bottom — 20 mm**.
- Page numbering — Arabic numerals, **continuous**, centered at the bottom of the page without a dot.
- The title page is included in the numbering, but the number is **not printed** on it.

## Dissertation structure (§4.1)

1. Title page (Appendix A of the Instruction; with the candidate's personal signature).
2. Contents.
3. Normative references ("This dissertation uses references to the following standards").
4. Definitions.
5. Designations and abbreviations (may be combined: "Definitions, designations and abbreviations").
6. Introduction.
7. Main body (sections → subsections → items → sub-items).
8. Conclusion.
9. List of references used (Appendix B; in order of appearance of references).
10. Appendices.

For details of each element's content — see [[../10-dissertation/structure]].

## Abstract structure (§4.2)

1. Title page (Appendix D) + reverse side (Appendix E).
2. Introduction (general characteristics of the work: relevance, aim, scientific novelty,
   statements for defense, approbation, practical value).
3. Main body.
4. Conclusion.
5. List of published works on the topic.
6. Summary (in two languages: English for all; Kazakh — for Russian-language works, Russian — for
   Kazakh-language works; 2 pages per language).
7. Imprint (output data).

> At the IITU council, a **trilingual annotation (up to 15 pp.)** is prepared for the defense —
> see [[../11-abstract-annotation/structure]] for its structure.

## Numbering and headings (§6.4)

- Sections — Arabic numerals without a dot, with a paragraph indent.
- Subsection = "section number.subsection number" (1.1, 1.2), no trailing dot.
- Item = "section.subsection.item" (3.1.1); sub-item (4.2.1.1).
- Headings — capitalized, no dot, not underlined.
- Each structural element — **on a new page**.

## Illustrations, tables, formulas (§6.5–6.8)

- **Figures**: continuous Arabic numbering; caption below: "Figure 1 – Title".
- **Tables**: title to the left above the table: "Table 1 – Title"; on continuation — "Continuation of Table 1".
- **Formulas**: on a separate line, number in parentheses at the right edge: `(1)`; symbols explained below.
- In-text references: "in accordance with Figure 2", "in formula (1)".

## References and sources (§6.9, §6.11)

- References to sources — in **square brackets** `[12]`, with the page on repeat.
- List of references — in order of appearance, Arabic numerals without a dot.
- Bibliographic description — per **GOST 7.1-2003**.

## Appendices (§6.12)

- Each appendix — on a new page, the word "Appendix" + a letter (A, B, V…, excluding certain letters).
- Continuous page numbering shared with the dissertation.
