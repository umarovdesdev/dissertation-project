To develop and experimentally validate an integrated fundus image enhancement and CNN-based classification framework for automated multi-stage diabetic retinopathy diagnosis, incorporating a 5-component preprocessing pipeline — FOV standardization, green channel imaging, pixel normalization, CLAHE enhancement in LAB color space with dynamic clip limit, and HSV contrast enhancement — to improve microvascular feature visibility and classification robustness; to validate the framework's transferability across multiple independent clinical datasets (EyePACS as primary training, with cross-database generalization on Messidor/Messidor-2 and IDRiD); to evaluate model robustness under image degradation and across diverse imaging devices (RFMiD, DDR, ODIR-5K); to demonstrate through Grad-CAM explainability analysis that preprocessing redirects CNN attention toward clinically relevant lesion structures; to formalize an external image ingestion protocol for integrating heterogeneous clinical data from sources outside standard benchmark datasets; and to accomplish these objectives under constrained computational conditions representative of resource-limited deployment environments.
