The proposed model is defined as a two-stage system: a 5-component fundus image preprocessing pipeline followed by a CNN classifier. The preprocessing pipeline is an integral component of the model, not an external data preparation step — it defines the feature space available to the CNN. This integrated preprocessing-CNN system — comprising FOV standardization, green channel imaging, pixel normalization, CLAHE enhancement in LAB color space with dynamic clip limit, and HSV contrast enhancement — reduces domain variability across fundus imaging devices and acquisition conditions while preserving diagnostically relevant retinal features, producing a statistically measurable and reproducible improvement in five-class diabetic retinopathy diagnostic performance compared to an equivalent architecture trained without preprocessing. This improvement, validated primarily on EyePACS with generalization demonstrated on Messidor/Messidor-2 and IDRiD, extends to robustness across diverse imaging devices (RFMiD, DDR, ODIR-5K capturing Topcon, Kowa, Canon, and Zeiss camera hardware), and is further substantiated by Grad-CAM explainability analysis showing that preprocessing redirects CNN attention toward clinically relevant lesion regions, quantified by Attention–Lesion Overlap (ALO) as the primary metric and IoU as a secondary metric. The pipeline thus constitutes a transferable, interpretable, and device-robust preprocessing regime for automated DR diagnosis under constrained computational conditions.
