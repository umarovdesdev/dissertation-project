To develop and experimentally validate an integrated fundus image enhancement and CNN-based classification framework for automated multi-stage diabetic retinopathy diagnosis, incorporating a 6-stage V4 preprocessing pipeline — canonical orientation normalization, FOV crop and resize, flat-field illumination correction, upgraded CLAHE enhancement in LAB color space with dual-constraint clip limit and stochastic application, and ImageNet-standard normalization — to improve microvascular feature visibility and classification robustness; to validate the framework's transferability across multiple independent clinical datasets (EyePACS as primary training, with cross-database generalization on Messidor/Messidor-2 and IDRiD); to evaluate model robustness across diverse imaging devices (RFMiD, DDR, ODIR-5K); to demonstrate through Grad-CAM explainability analysis that preprocessing redirects CNN attention toward clinically relevant lesion structures; to formalize an external image ingestion protocol for integrating heterogeneous clinical data from sources outside standard benchmark datasets; and to accomplish these objectives under constrained computational conditions representative of resource-limited deployment environments.
