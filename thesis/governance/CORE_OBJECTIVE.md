To develop and experimentally validate an integrated fundus image enhancement and CNN-based classification framework for automated multi-stage diabetic retinopathy diagnosis, incorporating an 8-stage preprocessing pipeline — canonical flip, OD-fovea rotation normalization, FOV crop with isotropic resize, FOV mask generation as a 4th input channel, adaptive flat-field illumination correction, upgraded CLAHE enhancement in LAB color space with dual-constraint clip limit and stochastic application, and dataset-specific channel-wise normalization — to improve microvascular feature visibility and classification robustness; to validate the framework's cross-dataset transferability (EyePACS as primary training, with zero-shot evaluation on APTOS 2019); to evaluate clinical degradation resistance on IDRiD and Messidor-2; to evaluate model robustness across diverse imaging devices (DDR, ODIR-5K, RFMiD); to demonstrate through Grad-CAM explainability analysis that preprocessing redirects CNN attention toward clinically relevant lesion structures, with quantitative ALO/IoU on IDRiD and qualitative Grad-CAM on a Kazakh clinical dataset; to validate trainability on small clinical data (IDRiD → Clinical); and to accomplish these objectives under constrained computational conditions representative of resource-limited deployment environments.
