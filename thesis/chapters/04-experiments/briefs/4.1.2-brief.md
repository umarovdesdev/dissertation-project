**Section objective:** Characterize the EyePACS class distribution (qualitatively + via FIG-4.1, no fabricated counts), show how that imbalance determines the metric and loss choices already specified in Chapter 3 (SC-1.4), specify the label-harmonization mapping across the architecture's taxonomies, and specify the patient-level stratified partitioning (reusing the §3.4.2 leakage framing).

**Argumentative spine (required):**
- **Thesis:** The training corpus's severe class imbalance is not a nuisance to be reported and forgotten but the structural fact that fixes three downstream design choices — the primary-metric hierarchy, the loss function, and the stratified partitioning — and the architecture's taxonomic heterogeneity imposes a label-harmonization step that must precede any cross-dataset use.
- **Reasoning chain:**
  1. Open from the SB-2.1 flag raised in §4.1.1: the EyePACS grade distribution is severely imbalanced (majority no-DR, small severe/proliferative minorities); show it via FIG-4.1; do not assert precise counts (SIR-1).

  3. Specify label harmonization (SB-2.3, SIR-3): the common target is the 5-class ICDR scale; Messidor-2's referable/non-referable + grade and the multi-disease RFMiD/ODIR-5K DR subsets are mapped onto it, with the documented caveat that Experiment 6 uses DR labels only (non-DR labels excluded or mapped to non-DR).
  4. Specify partitioning: patient-level 5-fold stratified CV (grade-stratified to preserve class proportions across folds; patient-level grouping prevents leakage; composes with train-only augmentation) — reuse §3.4.2 / FIG-3.13 framing, do not re-derive the leakage argument.
  5. Note IDRiD reports no class distribution (#06/#10, SIR-2) and that its native 413/103 split is the descriptor's, whereas Experiment 7 re-partitions IDRiD under the common 5-fold protocol.
- **Conclusion / hand-off:** The training tier is characterized and all data partitioned under one leakage-controlled protocol; hands off to §4.1.3 (hardware/reproducibility).
