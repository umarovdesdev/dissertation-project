### CONTENT SPECIFICATION

**Section objective:** Critically analyze the landscape of existing automated DR screening systems — from the landmark and FDA-cleared deployments through external, prospective, and meta-analytic validations — and surface the methodological gap (preprocessing treated as ancillary and chronically under-reported; heterogeneous and often single-dataset/single-country validation; fragile reproducibility; predominantly binary/opaque deployments) that the dissertation's controlled, preprocessing-formalized, multi-dataset design addresses — without claiming superiority over any named system.

**Argumentative spine (required):**
- **Thesis of this section:** Existing automated DR systems achieve high and sometimes clinically-validated reported performance, but the literature as a body exhibits a consistent methodological pattern — preprocessing treated as ancillary data preparation and left under-reported, validation that is frequently single-dataset or single-country, and reproducibility that degrades sharply on public data — and this pattern, not any performance deficit, is the gap the dissertation is designed to address.
- **Reasoning chain:**
  1. The field has produced landmark (#12) and FDA-cleared autonomous (#77) systems with high reported metrics, and has matured toward external (#11, #44, #07, #03), prospective (#45), and meta-analytic (#14) validation.
  2. Yet these results are mutually heterogeneous and non-comparable (SIR-3): binary referable-DR vs 4/5-class tasks, private vs public data, different countries/devices, different reference standards.
  3. Reproducibility is fragile: independent public-data reproduction degrades markedly (the Voets finding from §1.2.2), and systematic review identifies overfitting, class imbalance, and early-stage detection as recurring unresolved weaknesses (#39).
  4. Across these systems preprocessing is consistently treated as ancillary and under-reported (Gulshan defers to supplement; Zhang and Ruamviboonsuk do not report preprocessing) — the observable P1 practice (SIR-9) — so its contribution is neither formalized nor evaluated as a model component.
  5. Deployed/commercial systems are predominantly binary, opaque, single-disease, or hardware-bound (the candidate's prior comparative analysis, #22, of nine systems; #43 off-modality), limiting transparent, five-class, device-robust, preprocessing-formalized study.
  6. Therefore the gap is methodological: a controlled, transparent, multi-dataset evaluation that formalizes preprocessing as an integral model component and reports it fully — not a claim to outperform existing systems (CFC-2.2/NC-2).
- **Conclusion / hand-off:** Establishes the methodological gap as the motivation; hands off to §1.5, which formulates this gap into the explicit research problem and objectives.
