### CONTENT SPECIFICATION

**Section objective:** Establish the biological basis of diabetic retinopathy and the standard five-class clinical grading system (DR 0–4), defining the visible fundus lesions and the ordinal severity taxonomy that constitute the target classes for every downstream classifier in this dissertation.

**Argumentative spine (required):**
- **Thesis of this section:** Diabetic retinopathy is a progressive microvascular (with an early neurodegenerative) disease whose severity is defined by discrete, frequently subtle fundus lesions, and the standard five-class grading scale (DR 0–4) operationalizes this progression into the ordinal target taxonomy that an automated DR classifier must reproduce.
- **Reasoning chain:**
  1. DR originates in chronic-hyperglycemia–driven microvascular injury (pericyte loss → inner blood–retina-barrier breakdown), with evidence that retinal neurodegeneration may precede overt microvasculopathy (#32, #33, #34, #35).
  2. This pathology becomes visible on fundus images as a characteristic, ordered set of lesions — microaneurysms, dot/blot hemorrhages, hard exudates, cotton-wool spots, venous/intraretinal microvascular abnormalities, and ultimately neovascularization — plus diabetic macular edema as a vision-threatening overlay.
  3. The international clinical grading scale discretizes lesion burden into five ordinal severity levels (DR 0 none → DR 4 proliferative); these are the dissertation's target classes per IT-1 / the operational definitions.
  4. The defining early lesions (e.g., microaneurysms in DR 1–2) are small and low-contrast, and adjacent severity grades differ subtly — an ordinal structure with clinically unequal error costs.
- **Conclusion / hand-off:** Establishes the disease, its visible signs, and the DR 0–4 ordinal taxonomy as fixed clinical ground truth; licenses §1.1.2 to take the *need to detect this disease at scale* as given and to address screening burden and resource-limited settings.

**Required content elements:**
1. DR as a complication of diabetes mellitus; microvascular pathophysiology cascade (hyperglycemia → pericyte dropout → BRB breakdown → leakage/ischemia → neovascularization), with the neurodegeneration-may-precede-microvasculopathy nuance (#32, #33, #34, #35).
2. Catalogue of the visible fundus lesions defining DR and their pathological correlates (microaneurysms, hemorrhages, hard/soft exudates, IRMA, venous beading, neovascularization; diabetic macular edema).
3. The non-proliferative (NPDR) vs proliferative (PDR) dichotomy and the five-class ordinal severity scale DR 0–4 as the dissertation's target taxonomy (tie to IT-1 / OD scope).
4. Analytical observation that early-grade lesions are small/subtle and grade boundaries are ordinal with unequal misclassification costs — motivating (not proving) downstream image-quality and ordinal-task concerns.
5. Explicit framing that cited prevalence/screening/treatment statistics are third-party clinical context (CFC-2.3 / SIR-1), not dissertation results.
