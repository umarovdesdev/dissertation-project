### CONTENT SPECIFICATION

**Section objective:** Establish, from cited empirical evidence, that automated DR-classification performance is materially sensitive to image quality and data provenance — by margins that in places rival architectural differences — while preserving the honest nuances (aggregate quality-filtering can be performance-neutral; preprocessing is necessary-not-sufficient and task-dependent), thereby motivating the dissertation's stance of conditioning images in rather than gating them out.

**Argumentative spine (required):**
- **Thesis of this section:** Under OD-1, image quality is not a cosmetic preliminary but an empirically measured first-order determinant of automated DR-classification performance; the literature shows degraded or distributionally-shifted images systematically lowering measured performance — sometimes by margins comparable to architecture choice — yet also shows that simply discarding low-quality images is not a reliable performance lever, which is precisely why the dissertation conditions images rather than gating them.
- **Reasoning chain:**
  1. Direct evidence: holding architecture fixed, DR-detection accuracy declines as graded image quality declines (#78 EyeQ, Good→Usable→Reject).
  2. Quality as the parsimonious cross-dataset explanation: a model's higher score on a cleaner benchmark is attributed by the source to gradability, not generalization (#05 Rakhlin, Messidor-2 100% vs Kaggle ~75% gradable).
  3. Provenance/quality can dominate architecture: the *same* network yields markedly different performance across data sources (#17 Voets, AUC 0.951 → 0.853), implicating data conditions over architecture.
  4. Honest nuance #1 — filtering ≠ gain: excluding ~19.9% ungradable images did not significantly change performance (#17), so aggregate quality-gating is not a dependable lever.
  5. Honest nuance #2 — necessary-not-sufficient, task-dependent: automated image-quality grading itself is hard (κw/accuracy ≈ 0.65–0.70, "insufficient for clinical feasibility"), and a top DR-grading team succeeded with minimal preprocessing via training-strategy innovations (#15 Liu) — preprocessing's payoff depends on task and pipeline.
  6. Candidate's own prior evidence: preprocessing (resize + augmentation) raised validation accuracy from 71% to 86% on a small custom CNN, ROC-AUC 0.9638 (#24, prior own work, bounded by SIR-4/5/7).
- **Conclusion / hand-off:** Image quality is a measured, first-order factor, but the relationship is not a simple monotone filter-and-gain; this motivates integral image conditioning (geometry/illumination/contrast normalization) developed in Chapters 2–3, and hands off to §1.2.3, where one *systematic, structured* source of quality/distribution variation — the imaging device — is examined.

1. Restate OD-1 framing (performance-defined quality); connect to §1.2.1 degradation taxonomy as given.
