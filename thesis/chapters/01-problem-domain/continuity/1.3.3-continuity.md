**Key concepts established:**
- Explainability is the third methodological pillar (after architecture §1.3.1, pretraining §1.3.2); it addresses CNN opacity.
- XAI taxonomy/motivation: #59 Tjoa-Guan (perceptive vs mathematical-structure interpretability); #60 Samek (evaluation of explanations; LRP > sensitivity analysis; perturbation-based assessment).
- **Activation-mapping lineage** (the spatially-localizing family): CAM (#57, GAP-based, 37.1% top-5 loc error ILSVRC) → Grad-CAM (#46, gradient-weighted, any CNN, no retraining) → Grad-CAM++ (#58, better localization/multi-instance, avg-drop 36.8% vs 46.6%).
- **Attribution family as background**: SHAP (#61, additive feature attribution) and LIME (#62, local surrogate) — feature-importance, not spatial maps → retained as XAI background, NOT the operational tool.
- **Grad-CAM selected** as the dissertation's primary interpretability method: its spatial map → compared vs IDRiD pixel lesion masks → **ALO (primary) / IoU (secondary)** metrics in Exp 4 (§4.5).
- **NC-14 / SB-1.11 (binding):** Grad-CAM activation ≠ clinical localization of pathology and ≠ validation of diagnostic reasoning; it is post-hoc interpretability (gradient evidence), not pixel-accurate lesion delineation. High overlap is "consistent with" plausible attention, not proof of causation.
- All seven sources are general-domain (ImageNet/non-medical) method references; their metrics are method properties, not DR-lesion results (SIR-2/3).
