# CONTINUITY NOTE — §1.2.3 Device-Specific Variability in Fundus Imaging

**Version:** 6.0.0 · Produced as PART 2 of the §1.2.3 writing session · Carry into the §1.3.1 session (step c).

---

**Key concepts established:**
- Distribution shift in fundus imaging has two components: partly-**random** within-image degradation (§1.2.1) and **systematic, reproducible** device-determined variation (this section). The systematic component behaves differently — it is a coherent, repeatable transformation of the input distribution, i.e. domain shift.
- Between-device axes of difference (reproducible hardware properties): color rendition, illumination geometry (vignetting/central reflex), field-of-view angle, sensor resolution, optics.
- The dissertation's dataset architecture deliberately spans device heterogeneity: EyePACS/Canon CR-1 (primary training); RFMiD/Topcon TRC-NW300 + Kowa; DDR/Canon + Topcon; ODIR-5K/Canon + Zeiss. RFMiD & ODIR-5K are multi-disease → DR-subset must be mapped (SB-2.3).
- Domain-shift theory grounding: #74 (DG survey — i.i.d. assumption violated; OOD learner "suffers significant performance drops") and #75 (DA survey — discrepancy/adversarial/reconstruction method families). Both general/non-retinal → conceptual grounding only (SIR-1/2).
- Two motivated responses: preprocessing as between-device **standardization** (Chs 2–3) and an explicit **device-domain-shift experiment** (§4.7, H-6). Standardization narrows but cannot provably erase shift in resolution/optics/FOV → empirical experiment needed.
- **NC-16 boundary** stated as foreshadow: device-shift results = empirical cross-device observation, **not** certification/regulatory compliance; bounded to tested camera models.

**Terms introduced (now available without re-definition):** domain shift, out-of-distribution (OOD), domain generalization (DG), domain adaptation (DA, with discrepancy/adversarial/reconstruction taxonomy), device domain shift, camera models (Canon CR-1, Topcon TRC-NW300, Kowa, Zeiss), systematic-vs-random distribution shift, between-device standardization.

**Argument thread:** Device variability is the systematic, theory-grounded component of the distribution-shift problem — *probeable* (motivating §4.7) and *partly addressable by standardization* (motivating Chs 2–3), without overclaiming either, and explicitly not certification (NC-16). With this, the **clinical/technical problem characterization (Ch 1 §§1.1–1.2) is complete**, and the chapter pivots from problem to methods.
