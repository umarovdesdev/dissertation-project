**Version:** 6.0.0 · Produced as PART 2 of the §1.1.1 writing session · Carry into the §1.1.2 session (step c).

**Key concepts established:**
- Diabetic retinopathy (DR) as a chronic microvascular complication of diabetes mellitus, more fully framed as a disorder of the retinal **neurovascular unit** (microvascular damage + inflammation + retinal neurodegeneration).
- The microvascular pathophysiology cascade: chronic hyperglycemia → pericyte dropout → inner blood–retina-barrier breakdown → leakage / capillary non-perfusion / ischemia → VEGF-driven neovascularization.
- The convergent observation (across #32, #33, #34, #35) that retinal neurodegeneration **may precede** overt microvasculopathy.
- The visible fundus-lesion progression that DR grading scores: microaneurysms → dot/blot hemorrhages → hard exudates → cotton-wool spots → IRMA / venous beading → neovascularization; with diabetic macular edema (DME) as an independent vision-threatening overlay.
- The standard **five-class clinical grading scale DR 0–4** (DR 0 none, DR 1 mild NPDR, DR 2 moderate NPDR, DR 3 severe NPDR, DR 4 PDR), via the ETDRS and International Clinical DR (ICDR) scales — established as the dissertation's canonical classification target per IT-1.
- Two design-relevant properties of the taxonomy: it is **ordinal** (error cost scales with grade distance), and the screening-critical early-grade boundaries rest on **small, low-contrast** lesions.

**Terms introduced (now available without re-definition):** diabetic retinopathy (DR), non-proliferative DR (NPDR), proliferative DR (PDR), microaneurysm, intraretinal (dot/blot) hemorrhage, hard exudate, cotton-wool spot (soft exudate), intraretinal microvascular abnormality (IRMA), venous beading, neovascularization, diabetic macular edema (DME), pericyte, blood–retina barrier, retinal neurovascular unit, the **DR 0–4** five-class grading scale, ETDRS scale, ICDR scale.

**Argument thread:** The disease and its visible signs have been fixed, and the DR 0–4 ordinal taxonomy named as the classification target every downstream model must reproduce. The active thread closes on a clinical-need beat: the treatable therapeutic window exists only if DR is caught in its lower, subtle grades — which establishes a demand for accessible, reliable screening.
