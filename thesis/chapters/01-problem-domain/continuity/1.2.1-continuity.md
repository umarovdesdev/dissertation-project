# CONTINUITY NOTE — §1.2.1 Sources of Image Degradation in Clinical Practice

**Version:** 6.0.0 · Produced as PART 2 of the §1.2.1 writing session · Carry into the §1.2.2 session (step c).

---

**Key concepts established:**
- Image quality and degradation fixed on OD-1 terms: quality = measurable capacity of a fundus image to support automated detection of DR-relevant microvascular features (assessed via downstream classification under matched conditions); degradation = any acquisition-side erosion of that capacity.
- A four-axis taxonomy of acquisition-side degradation:
  1. **Optical** — defocus + motion blur act as low-pass filters, selectively erasing high-frequency small-lesion signal (microaneurysms).
  2. **Photometric** — uneven illumination, vignetting/shading, exposure error, low contrast; structured/spatially-correlated, can both mask and mimic lesions (threatens sensitivity and specificity).
  3. **Geometric** — off-axis capture, misalignment, FOV truncation, magnification variation; undermines landmark (OD/fovea) consistency and can exclude peripheral lesions.
  4. **Patient/media** — poor mydriasis, cataract/vitreous opacity, corneal reflections, dust/flare; partly patient-origin, not removable by camera engineering alone.
- **Central analytical pivot:** degradation is *target-correlated* — it preferentially attacks the small, low-contrast features defining the earliest, most treatable DR grades — which makes it a diagnostic threat, not a cosmetic one.
- Resource-limited/portable/non-specialist settings (§1.1.2) aggravate every axis → the settings where automated screening is most needed are where degradation is worst.
- Quality-control gating reframes (does not remove) the problem: it discards rather than recovers signal (lowering coverage), and sub-threshold degradation still erodes small-lesion signal.

**Terms introduced (now available without re-definition):** image degradation (four axes), defocus, motion blur, low-pass attenuation of high-frequency detail, vignetting/shading, central reflex, exposure error, off-axis/FOV truncation, mydriasis, media opacity (cataract/vitreous/corneal), corneal reflection/flare, quality-control gating, target-correlated degradation.
