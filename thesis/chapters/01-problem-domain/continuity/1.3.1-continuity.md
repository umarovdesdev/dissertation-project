**Key concepts established:**
- CNNs are the workhorse model class for automated DR classification; they learn a convolution/pooling feature hierarchy (early edge/texture → deep task-specific). FIG-2.2 schematizes this.
- Architecture lineage and its inherited innovations: AlexNet (#65: ReLU/dropout/PCA-color) → ResNet (#66: residual connections) → DenseNet (#67: dense reuse) → EfficientNet (#68: compound scaling) → EfficientNetV2 (#69). **ResNet-50 (#66) and EfficientNet-B3 (#68) named as the dissertation's two operational backbones.**
- DR-classification literature is high-performing but **heterogeneous** in dataset, class taxonomy (binary rDR vs 5-class DR 0–4), and validation rigor → reported numbers are **not directly comparable** (SIR-3); high accuracy under imbalance can hide low sensitivity (#01 Pratt 30% sens).
- Validation-rigor caveats applied: #42 Arora (~11-pt overfitting gap, no external val, SIR-7), #41 Sharma (class-AUC 0.44–0.56 incompatible with 94% acc — AUC not relied upon), #37 Geetha-Hema (no data/validation disclosed), #18 González-Díaz (AMD not DR).
- Same architectures generalize in *form* to adjacent tasks/modalities — marked off-task: #08 segmentation, #38 hemorrhage, #43 OCTA, #18 AMD (SB-1.4).
- Vision transformers (#70 "large-scale training trumps inductive bias"; #16 Swin>CNN on their data; #69 EfficientNetV2 CNN-competitive) addressed as the counter-position; **dissertation's CNN commitment is a deliberate design choice (CNN-native SSL, 4-channel input, two-family replication), NOT a superiority/optimality claim** (CFC-2.2/NC-6/SB-3.1).
- **Pivotal move:** architecture held constant (two backbones) → preprocessing/pretraining becomes the controlled experimental factor (EH-4 replication foreshadowed).

**Terms introduced (now available without re-definition):** convolutional neural network (CNN), convolution/pooling feature hierarchy, residual connection, dense connectivity, compound scaling, backbone, ResNet-50, EfficientNet-B3/B4, DenseNet, AlexNet, vision transformer (ViT), Swin transformer, inductive bias, referable DR vs five-class DR 0–4 (reinforced).

**Argument thread:** The literature offers many high-but-incomparable architecture results; rather than adjudicate, the dissertation fixes two well-characterized CNN backbones and varies preprocessing/pretraining — converting a contested architecture variable into a controlled one. The active thread closes by naming initialization/adaptation (transfer learning vs in-domain SSL) as the next performance determinant.

**Final topic:** The last paragraph fixes the two-backbone commitment (no optimality claim) and hands to §1.3.2 by stating that how a backbone is *initialized and adapted* is the next determinant and the locus of the V5-arm pretraining choice.
