# CONTINUITY NOTE — §1.2.2 Impact of Image Quality on Diagnostic Model Performance

**Version:** 6.0.0 · Produced as PART 2 of the §1.2.2 writing session · Carry into the §1.2.3 session (step c).

---

**Key concepts established:**
- Under OD-1, a demonstrated link between graded image quality and downstream metrics *is* a direct observation of the image-quality construct (not a proxy).
- Empirical convergence (with caveats) that image quality + data provenance are **first-order determinants** of automated DR-classification performance, sometimes rivaling architecture:
  - #78 EyeQ: architecture fixed, DR-detection accuracy declines Good→Usable→Reject (ResNet-50 0.9154/0.9150/0.9068).
  - #05 Rakhlin: Messidor-2 AUC 0.967 vs Kaggle 0.923, author attributes gap to gradability (~100% vs ~75%); P1-by-practice.
  - #17 Voets: *same* InceptionV3, AUC 0.951 (EyePACS) → 0.853 (Messidor-2) — provenance/label quality, not architecture.
- **Two load-bearing nuances** (guard CFC-2.1/2.8):
  1. Filtering ≠ gain — excluding ~19.9% ungradable images did not significantly change performance (#17): aggregate quality-gating is not a dependable lever.
  2. Necessary-not-sufficient & task-dependent — automated IQ grading itself only ~0.65–0.70 (#15); a top DR-grading team won with *minimal* preprocessing via training strategy.
- Candidate's prior work (#24, SIR-4): preprocessing (resize+aug) raised val acc 71%→86%, ROC-AUC 0.9638 on a small custom CNN — P2-tending precursor to V5; bounded by SIR-5 (one article, cite once) and SIR-7 (no architecture-class generalization).
- **Synthesized position:** *condition images in* (normalize geometry/illumination/contrast as an integral model component) rather than *gate images out* or assume any enhancement guarantees a gain.

**Terms introduced (now available without re-definition):** retinal image quality assessment (RIQA) / three-level quality grading (Good/Usable/Reject), gradability, referable DR (rDR, binary, distinct from 5-class DR 0–4), quadratic weighted kappa (κw), cross-dataset AUC drop / data provenance, "condition-in vs gate-out" framing, P2-tending prior self-work.

**Argument thread:** Image quality is a measured, first-order performance lever, but not a simple monotone filter-and-gain one — which motivates integral image conditioning (forward to OD-3 / Chapters 2–3). The active thread closes by isolating one *systematic, structured* source of the quality/distribution variation just evidenced: the imaging device.

**Final topic:** The last paragraph delivers the "condition-in over gate-out" synthesis, restates the CFC-2.1/2.8 discipline (no universal claim; no preprocessing-alone attribution), and hands to §1.2.3 by naming the imaging device as a systematic (non-random) source of distribution shift. Reader ends convinced quality matters but that the response must be conditioning, not gating, and primed to ask about between-device variation specifically.

Use dataset descriptors #47 (EyePACS/Canon), #49 (RFMiD/Topcon+Kowa), #50 (DDR/Canon+Topcon), #51 (ODIR-5K/Canon+Zeiss) for the camera-model evidence, and the domain-shift surveys #74 (DG) / #75 (DA) for the conceptual framing.
