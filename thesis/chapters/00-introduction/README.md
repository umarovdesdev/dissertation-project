## Paradigmatic framing insertion (v5.3) — Task 2.8

Binding content for when the Introduction is drafted:

- **Landmark mention.** Gulshan et al. (2016, *JAMA*) is to be introduced as the *landmark study that opened the era of CNN-based diabetic retinopathy screening*. This framing is permissible under SB-1.12 because it characterises the source's historical position, not its numerical figures.
- **Pre-introduction of the paradigmatic framing.** The Introduction must pre-introduce the P1 vs P2 contrast at a high level (one short paragraph), so that the reader arrives at chapter 1.4 already primed for the formal paradigmatic discussion. The pre-introduction is not the place to state the methodological-practice grounds in detail (those belong in chapter 1.4 and in the Gulshan literature card §15).
- **Forbidden phrasings** in the Introduction (per CFC-2.9, SB-1.12, CFC-2.2): "Gulshan is our baseline"; "we outperform Gulshan"; "Gulshan claimed preprocessing is unimportant"; "the present work surpasses Gulshan"; any framing that conflates Gulshan with the operational baseline of OD-3.
- **Permitted phrasings:** "Gulshan et al. (2016) is taken in this dissertation as the canonical representative of the end-to-end CNN paradigm (P1)"; "the present dissertation reframes preprocessing as an integral model component, operationalising the integrated preprocessing-CNN paradigm (P2)"; "the principal conceptual contribution of this work is the P1 → P2 paradigm shift, supported empirically by Experiment 1 under matched conditions."
