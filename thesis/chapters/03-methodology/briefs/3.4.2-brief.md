### CONTENT SPECIFICATION

**Section objective:** Specify the cross-validation and statistical-reliability protocols — 5-fold patient-level stratified cross-validation with leakage control, mean±std reporting, and the statistical-test suite (McNemar, DeLong, bootstrap 95% CIs, mixed-effects model, multiple-comparison correction) — together with the reproducibility conditions (TAB-3.1: fixed seed, deterministic mode, standardized hyperparameters), and relate them to the EH-3/EH-4 criteria they support.

**Argumentative spine (required):**
- **Thesis of this section:** Performance is estimated by 5-fold patient-level stratified cross-validation, reported as mean ± standard deviation, and compared by a statistical-test suite with multiple-comparison correction; together with fixed-seed determinism and a standardized training configuration, these protocols are what allow the EH-3/EH-4 verdicts to rest on quantified, reproducible uncertainty rather than point estimates.
- **Reasoning chain:**
  1. 5-fold patient-level stratified CV: 4 folds train / 1 test, repeated 5×; **patient-level** grouping prevents leakage (no patient's images in both train and test within a fold) — the precondition for honest generalization estimates (FIG-3.13; RESEARCH_ARCHITECTURE §2.2/§5.0). Augmentation (Stage 6) never in test (ties §3.1.3).
  2. Reporting: mean ± std across folds for all primary metrics.
  3. Statistical tests (RESEARCH_ARCHITECTURE §6.8): McNemar (paired classification, Exp 1); DeLong (ROC-AUC, Exp 1/3); bootstrap 95% CI (≥1000 resamples, all exp); mixed-effects model (fold as random effect, Exp 1); Bonferroni/Holm multiple-comparison correction (Exp 1/2).
  4. Reproducibility: seed 42, deterministic=true; standardized config (TAB-3.1); fixed augmentation params and LR schedule (RESEARCH_ARCHITECTURE §9.3); DGL-2 hardware-bound.
  5. Relation to EH-3/EH-4: the CIs and tests are what let a measured difference be declared dominance (EH-3) or sufficient validation (EH-4) rather than noise — restated, applied in §4.2.
- **Conclusion / hand-off:** With validation and statistics specified, the methodological body of Chapter 3 is complete; §3.C consolidates the reproducibility conditions.

### COUNTER-ARGUMENT & INHERITED LIMITATIONS

- **Counter-position:** that single-split evaluation or image-level splitting would be simpler. Answer: image-level splitting leaks correlated images of the same patient across train/test, inflating estimates; patient-level 5-fold CV with CIs is the protocol that makes the EH-3/EH-4 verdicts defensible. Concede reproducibility is bounded to the documented hardware (DGL-2) and that private-data dependence limits external reproducibility (SB-2.2, from §3.1.4).
