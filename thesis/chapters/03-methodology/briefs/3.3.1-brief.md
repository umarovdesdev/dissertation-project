### CONTENT SPECIFICATION

**Section objective:** Specify how the two primary backbones (§3.2.1) are adapted for the five-class DR task — replacement of the pretraining-task classification head with a five-way softmax head over DR 0–4, applied identically to ResNet-50 and EfficientNet-B3 across all configurations — so that the factorial contrast is read under a common output structure (EH-4), distinguishing this head adaptation from the pretraining source (§3.3.2) and the fine-tuning schedule (§3.3.3).

**Argumentative spine (required):**
- **Thesis of this section:** Both backbones are adapted to the five-class DR task by replacing their pretraining-task head with a common five-way softmax classification head; applying the identical adaptation to both architectures and all preprocessing arms is what keeps the Experiment 1 factorial a clean contrast and supplies the cross-architecture replication structure (EH-4).
- **Reasoning chain:**
  1. The pretrained backbones carry a head sized to their pretraining task; for DR the head is replaced with a five-way softmax over DR 0–4 (5-class, IT-1), with the feature extractor retained.
  2. The adaptation is identical across ResNet-50 and EfficientNet-B3 and across baseline/integrated arms — only preprocessing and pretraining source vary (the factorial); the head does not (SB-3.1, EH-4).
  3. This is distinct from (a) the initialization/pretraining source (§3.3.2) and (b) the fine-tuning schedule by which the adapted network is trained (§3.3.3); the prior frozen-vs-fine-tuned record (#19/#21, one thread; #23/#24 high-complexity 5-class softmax precedent) motivates the schedule but the adaptation itself is the head replacement.
  4. Transfer is not guaranteed (DGL-6); the adaptation is the mechanism by which transfer is attempted, evaluated empirically in Ch 4.
- **Conclusion / hand-off:** With the output structure fixed, §3.3.2 specifies the integrated-arm initialization (ophthalmology-specific SSL), and §3.3.3 the fine-tuning schedule.

**Required content elements:**
1. Head replacement (pretraining head → 5-way softmax over DR 0–4); feature extractor retained.
2. Identical adaptation across both backbones and both arms (factorial cleanliness; EH-4).
3. Distinction from pretraining source (§3.3.2) and fine-tuning schedule (§3.3.3); prior 5-class softmax precedent (#23/#24) and frozen/fine-tuned record (#19/#21).
4. DGL-6 (transfer not guaranteed).
