### CONTENT SPECIFICATION

**Section objective:** Specify the multi-metric assessment framework that operationalizes diagnostic effectiveness — the primary metrics in EH-1 order, the OD-5 effectiveness thresholds, the EH-2 secondary metrics (per-class, macro, training-overfitting), the clinical-screening metrics, calibration (ECE/Brier), image-quality metrics (CNR/VVI/Entropy/SSIM), the explainability metrics (ALO primary / IoU secondary), and the generalization ratio G — rendering TAB-3.2 and TAB-3.3 inline, with each metric's evidentiary role and scope (EH-1/EH-2, OD-4/OD-5, NC-14, SB-1.10) stated.

**Argumentative spine (required):**
- **Thesis of this section:** Diagnostic effectiveness is assessed by a hierarchy of metrics rather than any single number: weighted F1, ROC-AUC, and quadratic-weighted κ as primary evidence (with accuracy subordinate under imbalance), supported by secondary, clinical-screening, calibration, image-quality, and explainability metrics — each with a bounded evidentiary role — and by the cross-dataset generalization ratio G; the framework is designed so that no claim rests on a metric that is unstable or out of scope for it.
- **Reasoning chain:**
  1. Why multi-metric: under class imbalance accuracy is inflated; the evidence hierarchy (EH-1) ranks weighted F1 > ROC-AUC > quadratic-weighted κ > accuracy. [TAB-3.2]
  2. OD-5 diagnostic-effectiveness thresholds (Acc≥0.80, wF1≥0.80, ROC-AUC≥0.90, κ≥0.70), derived from previously published self-results (#24/LC-SAPAKOVA) as reference values (SIR-4; not transferable performance, SB-1.2/CFC-2.5).
  3. EH-2 secondary metrics: per-class P/R (unstable for minority), macro avg, training metrics (overfitting diagnosis only, 15-pp threshold OD-4) — cannot independently establish hypotheses.
  4. Clinical-screening metrics (sens/spec/PPV/NPV, referable DR ≥2) — reported for Exp 1/3/5.
  5. Calibration (ECE, Brier; #52 Guo) — empirical property, NOT clinical reliability (SB-1.10).
  6. Image-quality metrics (CNR, VVI, Entropy, SSIM; #53 SSIM) — quantify preprocessing effect independent of classification [TAB-3.3]; SSIM checks no-destructive-artifact (ties §3.1.1 design objective).
  7. Explainability metrics: ALO (primary) = area(GradCAM∩lesion)/area(lesion); IoU (secondary) = ∩/∪ (#64 PASCAL VOC IoU criterion borrowed as overlap metric) — NC-14: not clinical localization.
  8. Generalization ratio G = F1_external/F1_EyePACS (OD-4).
  9. These metrics feed EH-3 (dominance) / EH-4 (sufficient validation), evaluated in §4.2 — named, not applied.
- **Conclusion / hand-off:** With the metrics fixed, §3.4.2 specifies the cross-validation and statistical-reliability protocols that compute them with uncertainty.

### COUNTER-ARGUMENT & INHERITED LIMITATIONS

- **Counter-position:** that a single headline metric (e.g., accuracy) would suffice. Answer: under EyePACS imbalance accuracy is inflated and uninformative for minority grades; the hierarchy exists precisely so that dominance (EH-3) requires simultaneous improvement on weighted F1 AND ROC-AUC AND no κ degradation, which no single metric can fake. Concede each secondary metric's instability and bounded role (EH-2).
- **Inherited limitations (SIR-2):** #52/#64 are general method references (calibration/IoU on non-DR data); #24/#21 self thresholds STARE/APTOS/private; ALO/IoU bounded to IDRiD lesion masks (Exp 4) and to attention-overlap, not pathology localization (NC-14).
