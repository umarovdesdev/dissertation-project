### CONTENT SPECIFICATION

**Section objective:** Establish the four image-quality metrics — Contrast-to-Noise Ratio (CNR), Vessel Visibility Index (VVI), Image Entropy, and Structural Similarity (SSIM) — that quantify the preprocessing pipeline's effect on the image directly, independent of any classifier, and frame their expected behavior and evidentiary role (supplementary, EH-2) while flagging the thin, general-purpose nature of their sourcing.

**Argumentative spine (required):**
- **Thesis of this section:** Because the preprocessing pipeline is treated as a model component, its effect on the image must be measurable independently of downstream classification; four image-quality metrics — CNR and VVI (signal/structure visibility), Image Entropy (information content), and SSIM (structural preservation) — provide that classifier-independent view, with the first three expected to rise under the contrast/illumination stages and SSIM guarding against destructive over-enhancement, though all are supplementary evidence (EH-2) and drawn from general, not DR-specific, sources.
- **Reasoning chain:**
  1. Motivation: a classifier-independent measure of preprocessing effect is needed; downstream accuracy alone is confounded (and under H-1 jointly attributable, CFC-2.8). Image-quality metrics measure the image itself.
  2. CNR: contrast-to-noise ratio between vessel structures and background — quantifies signal separation; expected to rise after flat-field correction (Stage 4) and CLAHE (Stage 5).
  3. VVI: vessel visibility index — detectability of retinal vasculature, the most contrast-sensitive diagnostic structures; expected to improve under the same stages.
  4. Image Entropy: information-theoretic content of the intensity distribution; expected to increase after contrast enhancement as the intensity range is more fully used (glossary).
  5. SSIM (Wang et al., 2004): structural similarity between processed and original image; a high SSIM confirms enhancement preserves structure and introduces no destructive artifacts — a guard against over-enhancement.
  6. Evidentiary role and sourcing: these are supplementary metrics (EH-2), formalized in TAB-3.3 (§3.4.1) and applied in Exp 2 (§4.3.3); they are general-purpose metrics (SIR-1 THIN — #53 general image-quality, CNR/VVI without dedicated cards), and the calibration metrics (ECE/Brier, Guo et al., 2017) are a distinct model-level family, not image-quality metrics.
- **Conclusion / hand-off:** Image-quality metrics give a direct, classifier-independent view of the preprocessing component's effect (rising CNR/VVI/Entropy under structure-preserving SSIM), completing Chapter 2's theoretical foundations; §2.C synthesizes the chapter and distinguishes its experimentally-grounded strands from the theoretical (simulation-only) one.

### COUNTER-ARGUMENT & INHERITED LIMITATIONS

- **Counter-position:** "Higher image-quality metrics prove the preprocessing improves diagnosis." Refused: image-quality metrics are supplementary (EH-2) and measure image properties, not diagnostic performance; a rise in CNR/entropy does not establish a classification gain, and under H-1 the effect is jointly attributable (CFC-2.8). SSIM is included precisely because higher contrast can be destructive, so "more enhancement" is not automatically "better."
- **Inherited limitations (SIR-2):** #53 general image-quality, non-medical/non-DR; #52 calibration reference (different metric family); CNR/VVI without dedicated cards (THIN, SIR-1).
