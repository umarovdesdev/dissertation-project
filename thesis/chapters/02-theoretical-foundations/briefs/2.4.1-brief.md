### CONTENT SPECIFICATION

**Section objective:** Present the coupled optical (laser absorption) and thermal (heat conduction) mathematical model of laser-photocoagulation effects on fundus tissue, with its governing equations, as a standalone theoretical/simulation contribution — under strict bounding that it is qualitative, not clinically validated, and separate from the diagnostic-classification thesis.

**Argumentative spine (required):**
- **Thesis of this section:** A coupled optical–thermal model — Gaussian-beam incident intensity attenuated by Beer–Lambert absorption, feeding an energy-balance temperature source into a spatially varying heat-conduction equation solved by finite differences — provides a qualitative simulation of temperature distribution across heterogeneous fundus layers during laser photocoagulation, supporting (in principle) laser-parameter optimization; it is a theoretical contribution only, qualitatively evaluated and not clinically validated.
- **Reasoning chain:**
  1. Laser photocoagulation is a standard DR treatment; effective, tissue-sparing therapy requires predicting how laser energy distributes thermally across heterogeneous fundus layers — motivating a model (treatment-side, distinct from the diagnostic pipeline).
  2. Optical sub-model: incident Gaussian-beam intensity (Eq. 7) attenuated with depth by the Beer–Lambert law (Eq. 2), governed by an absorption coefficient β.
  3. Optical→thermal coupling: an energy-balance relation (Eq. 3) converts absorbed power to a local temperature rise (Eq. 4; small-depth simplification Eq. 5–6).
  4. Thermal evolution: the spatially varying heat-conduction equation (Eq. 8) with the temperature rise as initial condition, solved by an explicit finite-difference scheme (Python/NumPy/SciPy).
  5. Qualitative results: surface layers (cornea) heat rapidly, deeper layers (choroid, retina) slowly and stably, over 0–4.5 s — reported qualitatively, with no numerical temperature values; the source's effectiveness statement is the source's, not absorbed (SIR-6).
  6. Strict bounding: not clinically validated (SB-1.5, CFC-2.4); single self-source (SIR-1 THIN); model simplifications — no blood-perfusion (Pennes) term, static tissue properties, layered-homogeneity, fixed Gaussian profile (SIR-2).
