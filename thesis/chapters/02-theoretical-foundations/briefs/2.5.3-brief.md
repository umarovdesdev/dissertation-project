### CONTENT SPECIFICATION

**Section objective:** Formalize the two quantitative explainability metrics — Attention–Lesion Overlap (ALO, primary) and Intersection-over-Union (IoU, secondary) — that measure correspondence between a Grad-CAM map and pixel-level lesion masks, justify ALO's primacy given the coarse/class-discriminative nature of attention maps, and bind both as interpretability measures (NC-14), with IoU explicitly a borrowed overlap metric rather than a detection benchmark.

**Argumentative spine (required):**
- **Thesis of this section:** The attention-plausibility judgment of §2.5.2 is quantified by overlap between the Grad-CAM activation region and the annotated lesion mask; the Attention–Lesion Overlap (ALO), measuring the fraction of the lesion covered by attention, is the primary metric because it matches the interpretability question and tolerates the inherent coarseness of attention maps, while the symmetric Intersection-over-Union — borrowed from object detection — is retained as a stricter secondary measure; both are interpretability metrics, not detection benchmarks (NC-14).
- **Reasoning chain:**
  1. The need: turn "does attention fall on clinically relevant structures?" into a number by overlapping the (thresholded) Grad-CAM region with the lesion mask.
  2. ALO (primary): $\mathrm{ALO} = \dfrac{\mathrm{area}(\text{GradCAM} \cap \text{lesion})}{\mathrm{area}(\text{lesion})}$ — asymmetric, lesion-referenced; the fraction of the annotated lesion the attention covers; H-5's primary metric.
  3. IoU (secondary): $\mathrm{IoU} = \dfrac{\mathrm{area}(\text{GradCAM} \cap \text{lesion})}{\mathrm{area}(\text{GradCAM} \cup \text{lesion})}$ — symmetric; penalizes both missed lesion and attention spilling beyond it. Provenance in object detection: PASCAL VOC defines a correct detection as IoU > 0.5 (Everingham et al., 2010); GIoU (Rezatofighi et al., 2019) extends IoU to retain gradient for non-overlapping regions.
  4. Why ALO primary: Grad-CAM maps are coarse and class-discriminative (§2.5.1–§2.5.2) and systematically extend beyond precise lesion boundaries; a symmetric IoU penalizes that inherent coarseness as if it were localization error, whereas ALO measures lesion coverage — the interpretability question of interest. Hence ALO primary, IoU secondary/supporting.
  5. NC-14: both are interpretability measures; high ALO means attention covers the lesion, not that pathology was clinically localized; IoU is borrowed, not used as a detection threshold/benchmark (index note 47).
- **Conclusion / hand-off:** ALO (primary) and IoU (secondary) formalize attention-lesion overlap as interpretability measures, completing the explainability formalization (§2.5); the H-5 test (ALO_preproc > ALO_baseline) is reserved for Exp 4 (§4.5). The chapter's final theoretical strand — the image-quality metrics that evaluate preprocessing directly — follows in §2.6.
