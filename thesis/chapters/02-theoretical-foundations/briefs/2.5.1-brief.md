### CONTENT SPECIFICATION

**Section objective:** Formalize the class-activation-mapping lineage — CAM → Grad-CAM → Grad-CAM++ — culminating in the Grad-CAM equation used by the dissertation, under the strict non-claim (NC-14) that the resulting maps are gradient-weighted activation visualizations, not clinical lesion localization.

**Argumentative spine (required):**
- **Thesis of this section:** Class activation mapping localizes the image regions a CNN uses for a class prediction; CAM achieves this through global-average-pooling but requires a specific architecture, Grad-CAM generalizes it to any CNN by gradient-weighting the final convolutional feature maps, and Grad-CAM++ refines those weights — yielding a post-hoc, class-discriminative localization map that, crucially (NC-14), indicates gradient-weighted activation rather than pixel-level clinical lesion delineation.
- **Reasoning chain:**
  1. CAM (Zhou et al., 2016): a global-average-pooled final conv layer lets the classification weights be projected back onto spatial activations, yielding a class activation map $M_c(x,y)=\sum_k w_k^c f_k(x,y)$ — but it requires a GAP architecture and retraining.
  2. Grad-CAM (Selvaraju et al., 2017): generalizes CAM to any CNN without architectural change by weighting each feature map $A^k$ by the global-average-pooled gradient of the class score, $\alpha_k^c=\frac{1}{Z}\sum_{i,j}\frac{\partial y^c}{\partial A_{ij}^k}$, then $L^c=\mathrm{ReLU}\!\left(\sum_k \alpha_k^c A^k\right)$ — the ReLU retaining only features with positive influence on the class.
  3. Grad-CAM++ (Chattopadhyay et al., 2018): replaces the uniform gradient average with pixel-wise weighted gradients (higher-order terms), improving localization and multi-instance handling (reported average-drop 36.84% vs 46.56% on ImageNet) — a benchmark improvement, not a general superiority claim.
  4. NC-14: all three produce coarse, gradient-weighted activation maps at the final-conv resolution upsampled to the image; they indicate where the network's class evidence concentrates, NOT a pixel-level clinical delineation of lesion boundaries — a distinction binding on the dissertation's later overlap analysis.
- **Conclusion / hand-off:** The Grad-CAM formalization provides the interpretability map; how that map is *read* (and its inherent ambiguities) is §2.5.2, and how it is *quantified* against lesion masks (ALO primary, IoU secondary) is §2.5.3.
