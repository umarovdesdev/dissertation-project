### CONTENT SPECIFICATION

**Section objective:** Formalize the contrast stage's clip-limit rule — moving from the conventional contrast-limiting clip limit, through the candidate's prior single-threshold T/80 upgrade, to the **dual-constraint** clip limit `CL = min(clip_factor × tile_area/256, global_threshold × tile_area)` — explaining precisely what each constraint controls and why the minimum of the two is the design adopted, with TAB-2.1 contrasting the three formulations and Eqs. (2.1)–(2.3) stated.

**Argumentative spine (required):**
- **Thesis of this section:** The contrast stage adopts a dual-constraint clip limit that takes the minimum of a histogram-relative term (a multiple of the uniform per-bin count) and a tile-relative global cap (a fraction of the tile's pixel count); this bounds noise amplification under both near-uniform and strongly peaked local histograms, generalizing both the conventional single-factor clip and the candidate's prior single-threshold T/80 formulation, whose parameters are not assumed portable and are validated empirically in Experiment 2.
- **Reasoning chain:**
  1. The clip limit (from §2.1.1) is the height at which each tile's histogram is truncated before the cumulative mapping is formed; the conventional contrast-limiting clip sets it as a multiple (clip factor) of the uniform per-bin count tile_area/L (Eq. 2.1; #54 grounds the slope-limiting equivalence).
  2. The candidate's prior work replaced the conventional fixed/derived clip with a single global-threshold control, CL = T/80 (Eq. 2.2; #23/#24, self) — simpler, but governed by one parameter and validated only on STARE.
  3. A single per-bin factor over-amplifies when the local histogram is strongly peaked (large near-uniform background tiles, where tile_area/256 is a poor proxy for the true average occupied-bin height), while a single absolute cap ignores the local distribution; the rule takes the minimum of both terms (Eq. 2.3), so the tighter constraint binds per tile.
  4. The two parameters (clip_factor, global_threshold) are tunable and are characterized by the Experiment 2 sweep (§4.3.2); portability of any specific value across datasets/architectures is not assumed (DGL-5).
- **Conclusion / hand-off:** The contrast stage is now fully specified as a parameterized, dual-constraint operation on the LAB L-channel applied stochastically at train time (p=0.8) and deterministically at inference (OD-3 Stage 5); §2.1.3 turns to the complementary spatial-filtering/noise-reduction foundations.
