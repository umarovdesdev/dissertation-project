### CONTENT SPECIFICATION

**Section objective:** Establish how a Grad-CAM/attention map may legitimately be read — as a class-discriminative attention signal — and the four interpretive limits (coarse resolution, layer/architecture dependence, class-discriminativeness, and the activation≠pathology gap) that bound interpretation strictly below clinical localization (NC-14).

**Argumentative spine (required):**
- **Thesis of this section:** An attention map legitimately indicates the image regions that most influenced the predicted class, but its interpretation is bounded by coarse spatial resolution, dependence on the chosen layer and weighting method, its class-discriminative (not exhaustive) nature, and the irreducible gap between concentrated activation and the presence of pathology — so it supports judgments of attention plausibility, never clinical localization (NC-14).
- **Reasoning chain:**
  1. Legitimate reading: the map shows where the network's evidence for the predicted class concentrates (class-discriminative regions; #57/#46).
  2. Resolution limit: computed at final-conv resolution and upsampled, so boundaries are approximate; fine lesions may be attended without being precisely delimited.
  3. Method/layer dependence: the map depends on the layer and the weighting scheme — Grad-CAM++ (#58) changes localization by reweighting gradients — so interpretation is method-relative, not an absolute property of the model.
  4. Activation ≠ pathology (NC-14): concentrated attention over a region is not a determination that pathology is there; the network may attend to co-occurring context, and overlap with lesions is suggestive, not probative.
- **Conclusion / hand-off:** Interpretation is bounded to attention plausibility; turning the attention–lesion relationship into a number requires explicit overlap metrics (ALO primary, IoU secondary), themselves to be read as interpretability measures — the subject of §2.5.3.

**Required content elements:**
1. The legitimate reading (class-discriminative attention).
2. Coarse-resolution limit.
3. Layer/method dependence (Grad-CAM++ contrast).
4. Activation≠pathology gap (NC-14), overlap as suggestive not probative.
5. Hand-off to §2.5.3 (quantification).
