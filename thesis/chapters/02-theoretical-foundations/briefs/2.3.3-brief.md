### CONTENT SPECIFICATION

**Section objective:** Establish the theoretical rationale for ophthalmology-specific self-supervised pretraining of the CNN backbone as the integrated arm's initialization, situating it in the SSL design space and bounding the claim (DGL-6, CFC-2.8).

**Argumentative spine (required):**
- **Thesis of this section:** In-domain self-supervised pretraining on an unlabelled retinal corpus is theoretically motivated to yield more label-efficient, clinically generalizable backbone initialization than natural-image (ImageNet) pretraining — a motivation, not a guarantee (DGL-6), and within H-1 only one component of the integrated configuration (CFC-2.8).
- **Reasoning chain:**
  1. Label scarcity in medical imaging motivates learning from unlabelled data (#73, #92).
  2. The SSL design space — contrastive (#86, #87), negative-free (#88, #91), self-distillation (#89), masked-generative (#90) — supplies label-free pretext objectives, demonstrated on natural images.
  3. In-domain SSL beats ImageNet-supervised transfer in medicine (#85, non-retinal) and on retina specifically (#84, the keystone).
  4. Architecture nuance: the CNN-native in-domain path rests on the ResNet-compatible contrastive/negative-free family + MICLe; RETFound/MAE/DINO are ViT.
  5. The benefit is empirical, not guaranteed (DGL-6); within H-1 it is composite (CFC-2.8).
- **Conclusion / hand-off:** §2.3.3 fixes the theoretical basis; the operational pretraining procedure is specified in §3.3.2; empirical evaluation is deferred to Chapter 4 (H-1).

**Required content elements:**
1. Limited-label framing and the SSL principle (learn from unlabelled data, then fine-tune).
