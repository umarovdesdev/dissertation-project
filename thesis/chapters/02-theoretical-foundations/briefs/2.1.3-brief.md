### CONTENT SPECIFICATION

**Section objective:** Establish the theoretical foundations of edge-preserving spatial filtering and noise reduction — bilateral filtering and non-local means — as the formalization of the noise-suppression-versus-structure-preservation trade-off that constrains low-level fundus preprocessing, and clarify how the V5 pipeline addresses that trade-off without adopting either method as an explicit stage.

**Argumentative spine (required):**
- **Thesis:** The central constraint on noise reduction in fundus imaging is that the diagnostically decisive features (microaneurysms, thin vessels) are themselves small and low-contrast, so any smoothing must be content-adaptive to avoid erasing signal; bilateral filtering and non-local means are the canonical formalizations of edge-/structure-preserving denoising, and the V5 pipeline honors their design principle through contrast-limiting (Stage 5) and flat-field correction (Stage 4) rather than by inserting a dedicated denoiser.
- **Reasoning chain:**
  1. Naive (linear, domain-only) smoothing reduces noise but blurs edges and erases low-contrast detail — unacceptable when the target lesions are themselves low-contrast.
  2. Bilateral filtering (Tomasi & Manduchi, 1998) resolves this by combining geometric closeness with photometric similarity, so only perceptually similar values are averaged and edges are preserved.
  3. Non-local means (Buades et al., 2011) generalizes the similarity notion from spatial neighborhoods to patch self-similarity across the image, averaging non-local but appearance-similar patches.
  4. Both are qualitative method papers (no quantitative/retinal validation, SIR-2); they establish a *principle* (content-adaptive, structure-preserving operation), not evidence of DR gains (SIR-1).
- **Conclusion / hand-off:** The principle constrains the V5 design — the contrast stage limits (rather than amplifies) noise via the clip limit, and illumination is handled by flat-field correction — so bilateral/NLM stand as theoretical backdrop; the chapter now turns from low-level image operations to the CNN operations that consume their output (§2.2.1).

**Required content elements:**
1. The noise-vs-structure trade-off framed for fundus imaging (low-contrast targets).
2. Bilateral filtering: joint geometric + photometric weighting; edge preservation; (optional) the filter expression inline.
3. Non-local means: patch self-similarity; non-local averaging.
4. SIR-1/SIR-2 framing (qualitative, foundational, no DR evidence).
5. Relation to V5: principle honored via Stage 4 (flat-field) + Stage 5 (contrast-limiting clip), not via a dedicated denoiser; explicit non-adoption.

**Required tables/figures:** None.
**Required equations:** None mandated. The bilateral-filter integral may be stated inline for rigor.
