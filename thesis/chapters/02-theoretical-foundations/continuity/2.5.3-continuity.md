**Key concepts established:**
- **ALO (primary, H-5)**: $\mathrm{ALO}=\dfrac{\mathrm{area}(\text{GradCAM}\cap\text{lesion})}{\mathrm{area}(\text{lesion})}$ — asymmetric, lesion-referenced fraction of the lesion covered by attention.
- **IoU (secondary)**: $\mathrm{IoU}=\dfrac{\mathrm{area}(\text{GradCAM}\cap\text{lesion})}{\mathrm{area}(\text{GradCAM}\cup\text{lesion})}$ — symmetric; provenance in object detection (PASCAL VOC IoU>0.5, #64; GIoU, #63).
- **ALO primacy justified**: coarse, class-discriminative Grad-CAM maps extend beyond lesion boundaries by construction; symmetric IoU penalizes that coarseness as error, ALO measures lesion coverage → ALO primary, IoU a stricter secondary check (low IoU + high ALO = method-resolution property, not localization failure).
- **NC-14 / borrowed-not-benchmark (index note 47)**: both are interpretability measures, NOT detection/clinical-localization; IoU borrowed as overlap measure, not the detection threshold it originally defined.
- H-5 condition $\mathrm{ALO}_{\text{preproc}}>\mathrm{ALO}_{\text{baseline}}$ reserved for Exp 4 (§4.5).

**Terms introduced (now available without re-definition):** Attention–Lesion Overlap (ALO), Intersection-over-Union (IoU), lesion mask, thresholded activation region, symmetric vs asymmetric overlap, Generalized IoU (GIoU), overlap-as-interpretability vs detection-benchmark.
