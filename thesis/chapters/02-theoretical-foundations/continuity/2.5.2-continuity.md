**Key concepts established:**
- **Legitimate reading** of an attention map: class-discriminative activation — where the network's evidence for the predicted class concentrates; supports a judgment of **attention plausibility**.
- **Three interpretive limits**: (1) coarse final-conv **resolution** (boundaries approximate; fine lesions attended-not-delimited); (2) **method/layer dependence** (Grad-CAM++ reweighting changes localization → maps are a model-and-method property, comparable only with method fixed); (3) **activation ≠ pathology gap** (NC-14) — concentrated activation is not a pathology determination; the network may attend co-occurring context.
- **Overlap with lesions = suggestive, not probative**; the "where did class evidence concentrate / is it plausible?" question (interpretability) is distinct from "where is the pathology?" (clinical determination).
