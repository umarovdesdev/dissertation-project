**Key concepts established:**
- The contrast-enhancement lineage as a three-step progression: **global histogram equalization** (CDF-based intensity redistribution; globality is its limitation for background-dominated fundus images) → **adaptive histogram equalization (AHE)** (per-tile mapping recovers locality; failure mode = noise over-amplification via steep transformation slope in low-variance regions) → **contrast-limited / clipped adaptive histogram equalization** (Pizer et al. 1987: slope-limiting ≡ histogram clipping + redistribution; the methodological origin of CLAHE).
- The **clip limit** as the single governing control of CLAHE — sets the contrast-gain vs. noise-suppression trade-off. (Formal dual-constraint definition is deferred to §2.1.2; the glossary `[DEFINITION REQUIRED]` flags for "clip limit"/"tile grid" are to be resolved there.)

- Empirical contrast-enhancement effect is **real but contingent**: parameter- and architecture-dependent (Hayati ResNet34 −12.02% counterexample), not universally beneficial — motivating a formalized, swept parameterization rather than a presumed-beneficial fixed step.
- DGL-5 introduced: CLAHE parameter values are not asserted portable across image distributions/architectures.
