**Key concepts established:**
- The **noise-suppression vs structure-preservation trade-off** as the central constraint on low-level fundus preprocessing (low-contrast targets — microaneurysms, thin vessels — make indiscriminate smoothing dangerous).
- **Bilateral filtering** (Tomasi & Manduchi, 1998): similarity-weighted averaging combining geometric closeness $c(\xi-x)$ and photometric similarity $s(f(\xi),f(x))$; non-iterative, edge-preserving.
- **Non-local means (NLM)** (Buades et al., 2011): patch self-similarity averaging across the whole image; exploits structural redundancy to denoise while keeping fine detail.
- Both are **qualitative, foundational method papers** (no quantitative/retinal validation) — cited for principle, not DR gains (SIR-1/SIR-2).
- **V5 non-adoption**: neither is a pipeline stage; the noise-vs-structure trade-off is instead managed by the contrast-limiting clip (Stage 5) and adaptive flat-field correction (Stage 4) — design rationale, not a superiority claim.

**Terms introduced (now available without re-definition):** linear/domain-only smoothing, bilateral filtering, geometric closeness, photometric similarity, edge-preserving smoothing, non-local means (NLM), patch self-similarity, content-adaptive filtering, structural redundancy.
