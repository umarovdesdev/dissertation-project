**Key concepts established:**
- **Layer-dependent transferability** (Yosinski #71): lower layers learn generic structure (edges/colors/textures) and transfer near-perfectly; higher layers specialize; transferability **decays with source–target domain distance**; fine-tuned transferred features can beat training on the target alone.
- **Source-quality → transfer** (Kornblith #72): ImageNet accuracy predicts transfer (r=0.99 fixed, 0.96 fine-tuning); **fine-tuning > fixed-feature transfer** (179/192); ~17× convergence speed-up; but benefits diminish with divergence from source labels and more target data.
- **Natural-image→fundus = large domain gap**: ImageNet transfer to DR is a cross-domain shift (limited-label medical regime, #73); transfer useful but bounded (#04: AUC 0.94 EyePACS / 0.89 Messidor-2 from 2% labels).
- **DGL-6 two regimes**: baseline arm = ImageNet (cross-domain); integrated arm = ophthalmology-specific in-domain SSL of the same CNN backbone (reduces domain gap); transfer **not guaranteed, evaluated empirically**.
- **CFC-2.8**: integrated arm differs in both preprocessing AND pretraining axes → no isolated-axis attribution of any future H-1 effect.
- In-domain SSL theory itself is **DEFERRED to §2.3.3** (named only here).
