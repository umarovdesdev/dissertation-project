**Key concepts established:**
- **Class activation mapping (CAM)** (Zhou #57): GAP-based projection of class weights onto final-conv activations, $M_c(x,y)=\sum_k w_k^c f_k(x,y)$; requires a GAP architecture; weakly-supervised localization < fully supervised.
- **Grad-CAM** (Selvaraju #46, index-only/no card): gradient generalization — neuron-importance $\alpha_k^c=\frac{1}{Z}\sum_{i,j}\partial y^c/\partial A_{ij}^k$, map $L^c=\mathrm{ReLU}(\sum_k \alpha_k^c A^k)$; applies to any CNN without retraining; reduces to CAM for GAP nets; ReLU keeps positive-influence features.
- **Grad-CAM++** (Chattopadhyay #58): pixel-wise weighted (higher-order) gradients; better localization/multi-instance (avg-drop 36.84% vs 46.56%, ImageNet) — source benchmark, NOT an adopted ranking (CFC-2.2).
- **NC-14 (central, binding)**: Grad-CAM maps are coarse final-conv gradient-weighted activations upsampled for display — interpretability visualization, NOT pixel-level clinical lesion delineation; overlap with lesion masks = attention signal, not proof of clinical localization/reasoning.
