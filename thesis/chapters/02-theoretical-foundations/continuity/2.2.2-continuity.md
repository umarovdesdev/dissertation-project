**Key concepts established:**
- **Severe class imbalance** of DR data (no-DR ~73.5%; DR 3/4 ~2–2.5% in the self-work APTOS data; <3% classes 3–4 in #01) as the dominant constraint on loss design.
- **Softmax cross-entropy** and its failure under imbalance: majority-class gradient dominance → minority (severe-grade) under-learning + accuracy inflation + overfitting (train F1 0.87 vs test 0.62).
- **Cost-sensitive / class-weighted loss**: inverse-frequency / cost reweighting raises minority-error gradient; distribution-preserving and efficient vs resampling (#40), but thin field validation (2/173).
- **Focal loss** (adopted loss): modulating factor down-weights easy/majority examples, concentrates on hard/minority; γ=2, α=inverse-frequency — established technique, **formalization deferred to §3.3.4** (FIG-3.12); canonical source absent from corpus (flagged).
- **Imbalance-appropriate metrics**: weighted F1, ROC-AUC, quadratic-weighted Cohen's κ (EH-1, SC-1.4); accuracy demoted; κ's quadratic weighting matches the DR ordinal/unequal-cost structure (§1.1.1).
- SIR-5 (#19/#21 = one thread) and SIR-7 (no EfficientNetB0→efficient-CNN-class generalization) applied.
