**Version:** 6.0.0 · Carry into §6.4.1. Carry-in was 6.3.1-continuity (reading-centre review surface).

---

**Key concepts established:** the decision-support interface (realizing FR-5) that presents the FR-3 grade + FR-4 Grad-CAM overlay to a clinician; the overlay's bounded reading as a model-sensitivity map, NOT lesion localization (NC-14); the binding scope commitment that the system is **decision-support, physician-in-the-loop, NOT standalone diagnosis** (SB-1.3) — the clinician is the decision-maker who interprets/audits/overrides; the override-and-audit channel as the architectural counterpart of SB-1.3 (clinical accountability stays with the physician); the bidirectional Doctor-AI feedback loop (#22 EB-05); the persistence consequence — the Diagnostic Result entity records the clinician's disposition (accept/amend/override), so the stored authoritative outcome is the reviewed result, not the raw model output.

**Terms introduced (framing):** decision-support interface / review surface; clinician disposition (accept/amend/override) as the persisted outcome. Carried, already defined: physician-in-the-loop, Doctor-AI feedback loop (#22 EB-05); Grad-CAM overlay (NC-14, §6.2.2); Diagnostic Result / ER entities (§6.1.2).

**Argument thread:** the interface is the point at which the model's proposal becomes a clinician-owned decision, and the design fixes that ownership in what is persisted, not merely displayed. Active claim at section end: the system is a physician-in-the-loop decision-support surface; usability/acceptance evidence reserved (SB-4.1).

**Final topic:** the records the interface reads/writes are patient data traversing the storage/exchange layer; explicit hand-off to §6.4 (data management + protection protocols).
