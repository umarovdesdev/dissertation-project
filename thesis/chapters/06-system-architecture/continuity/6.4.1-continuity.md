# CONTINUITY NOTE — §6.4.1 GDPR/HIPAA-Aligned Data Management Protocols

**Version:** 6.0.0 · Carry into §6.4.2. Carry-in was 6.3.2-continuity (records incl. clinician disposition; reframe obligation for #22 p.90).

---

**Key concepts established:** the data-management protection protocols governing the system's patient records — encryption at rest and in transit, authentication, role-based access control, de-identification, audit logging (of access + clinician disposition), data minimization/retention, and a data-subject-request channel; their mapping to shared GDPR/HIPAA principles (confidentiality, integrity, access/purpose limitation, accountability) as the design-level realization of NFR-7; the store-and-forward consequence (queued-at-rest data must be encrypted locally; asynchronous exchange must be mutually authenticated/encrypted — protection must hold under intermittent connectivity); and the **central boundary that this is GDPR/HIPAA-*aligned* by design, NOT certified/audited/tested** (SB-4.2; NC-9). The prior publication's p.90 "GDPR and HIPAA-compliant" conclusion was explicitly **reframed** as a design requirement (not repeated), per #22 §V.2.

**Terms introduced (framing):** GDPR/HIPAA-aligned (vs -compliant); encryption at rest/in transit; role-based access control; de-identification; audit logging; data minimization/retention; data-subject request. Carried, already defined: security gateway, DMP, NFR-7 (§6.1.1/§6.1.2); store-and-forward (§6.1.2); Diagnostic Result/clinician disposition (§6.3.2).

**Argument thread:** alignment is a design requirement the architecture is built to satisfy, not a compliance status; no audit/certification/DPIA/pen-test conducted. Active claim at section end: the protocols are the design the system would have to implement and then have independently assessed to claim compliance — the claim of the section is the specification, not the compliance.

**Final topic:** jurisdiction-specific conformance — including the national data-protection regime applicable to a Kazakhstan deployment — is a design target reserved; explicit hand-off to §6.4.2.
