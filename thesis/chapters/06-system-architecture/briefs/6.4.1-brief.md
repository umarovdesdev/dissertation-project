### CONTENT SPECIFICATION

**Section objective:** Specify the data-management and protection protocols governing the patient data the system stores and exchanges (the §6.3.2 records and the §6.1.2 integration layer) as a GDPR/HIPAA-*aligned* design specification — at protocol level but strictly requirement-level and uncertified — landing the security deferrals from §6.1.2 and §6.3.1.2.

**Argumentative spine (required):**
- **Thesis:** The system's data-management layer is specified with protection mechanisms (encryption, authentication, access control, anonymization, audit logging, secure exchange) designed to *align with* GDPR and HIPAA principles, but this alignment is a design requirement the architecture is built to satisfy, not a certified, audited, or tested compliance status (SB-4.2; NC-9).
- **Reasoning chain:**
  1. Open from §6.3.2 / §6.1.2: the records and the integration layer carry patient data; restate design-only. State SB-4.2 spine.
  2. Specify the protection mechanisms (from #22 EB-04 / EB-06 DMP data-security): security gateway, encryption at rest and in transit, user authentication, role-based access control, anonymization/de-identification, audit logging of access and clinician disposition.
  3. Map these to GDPR/HIPAA design principles (confidentiality, data minimization, purpose limitation, access control, integrity/auditability) — as design requirements (NFR-7 realized).
  4. The store-and-forward consequence: queued data at rest on the local host must be encrypted and the asynchronous exchange authenticated/encrypted in transit — the protection design must hold under intermittent connectivity, not assume a trusted continuous channel.
  5. **Reframe and bound:** the prior publication concluded the system "is GDPR and HIPAA-compliant"; here that is reframed as a *design requirement*, because no compliance audit, certification, data-protection impact assessment, or penetration test has been conducted (SB-4.2; NC-9; CFC-2.4). Jurisdiction-specific conformance (including the applicable national regime) is a design target reserved — §6.4.2.
- **Conclusion / hand-off:** protection protocols fixed as a GDPR/HIPAA-aligned design spec; hands off to §6.4.2 (jurisdiction/Kazakhstan applicability).
