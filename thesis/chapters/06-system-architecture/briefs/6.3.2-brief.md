### CONTENT SPECIFICATION

**Section objective:** Specify the physician-in-the-loop interface (realizing FR-5) that presents the inference module's grade and Grad-CAM overlay to a clinician for interpretation, audit, and override — fixing, as the architecture's governing constraint, that the clinician is the decision-maker and the system is decision-support, not autonomous diagnosis.

**Argumentative spine (required):**
- **Thesis:** The interface realizes FR-5 by presenting the grade + overlay as *inputs to a clinical decision the physician makes and remains accountable for*; the physician-in-the-loop arrangement (interpret/audit/override + a Doctor-AI feedback channel) is a binding design commitment (SB-1.3), not an optional usability feature.
- **Reasoning chain:**
  1. Open from §6.3.1.1 reading-centre surface; restate design-only.
  2. Specify the interface contents: the five-class grade (FR-3) and the post-hoc Grad-CAM overlay (FR-4), with the overlay read as a model-sensitivity map supporting interpretation — NC-14 (not lesion localization).
  3. Fix SB-1.3: outputs are inputs to a decision; the clinician interprets, audits, and may override; the system does not issue an autonomous diagnosis. Ground in #22 EB-05 (physician as "tuner"/auditor; Doctor-AI feedback loop).
  4. Design rationale: the override/audit channel locates clinical accountability with the physician and is the architectural counterpart of the decision-support scope boundary; the feedback channel lets the clinician request clarification.
  5. Bound: no usability, acceptance, or clinical-decision-quality result is claimed (design-only, SB-4.1; no field test).
- **Conclusion / hand-off:** interface fixed as a decision-support surface; hands off to §6.4 (data management/compliance for the records the interface reads and writes).

**Required content elements:**
1. Design-only + SB-1.3 restatement up front.
