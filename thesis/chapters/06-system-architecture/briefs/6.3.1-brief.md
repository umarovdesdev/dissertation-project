### CONTENT SPECIFICATION

**Section objective:** Specify the deployment of the local grade-producing path (§6.2) in distributed telemedicine, national-eHealth-integrated, and low-resource real-time settings — as a design specification whose feasibility is contextualized by external deployment precedent, with no field-tested or projected outcome asserted as a finding of this work.

**Argumentative spine (required):**
- **Thesis:** The asynchronous, host-budgeted inference path of §6.2 is, by design, deployable in distributed/portable rural settings: the store-and-forward topology accommodates intermittent connectivity and the portable-capture-fed Ingestion module accommodates non-clinic acquisition — a design feasibility (SB-4.1/SB-4.3), supported by external precedent that DL DR screening operates in telemedicine/LMIC workflows, but not itself field-tested.
- **Reasoning chain:**
  1. Intro: take the §6.2 local path as the deployed unit; restate design-only + no field testing (SB-4.1, SB-4.3).
  2. **6.3.1.1 Distributed telemedicine:** the Ingestion module + store-and-forward (§6.1.2) realize remote diagnostic support across sites on intermittent links. External precedent: DL DR screening has been evaluated in primary-care/LMIC telemedicine workflows (#14 meta-analysis — pooled performance with prevalence-dependent PPV caveat and authors' caution against general recommendation; #45 prospective national-programme deployment) — cited as feasibility precedent with dataset/validation context (SIR-3, SIR-2), NOT as this system's results, NOT echoing specialist-superiority (CFC-2.2). #22 EB-03 telemedicine context (UK 80% coverage = external benchmark).
  3. **6.3.1.2 National eHealth integration:** the §6.1.2 PACS/EHR FHIR/HL7 layer is the integration point with a national eHealth infrastructure; Kazakhstan infrastructure context (#22 p.86–87). Infrastructure prerequisites acknowledged (DGL-4; #22 §II.6 / its stated need for infrastructure investment). SIR-8: Kazakhstan figures are projections.
  4. **6.3.1.3 Real-time remote screening in low-resource regions:** the OD-6 envelope *is* the low-resource regime; local inference + async make real-time-at-edge a **design feasibility, not a demonstrated capability** (SB-4.1/SB-4.3). Screening-gap motivation (#34: 35–55% compliance). Portable cameras feed the Ingestion module (#22 EB-03: portable retinal camera context, e.g. Fundus on Phone — external cost figure cited as context, not a claim).
  5. Close: feasibility-as-design summary; hand-off to §6.3.2 (physician-in-the-loop interface).
- **Conclusion / hand-off:** deployment design fixed (feasibility, not demonstrated); §6.3.2 specifies the clinician interface.
